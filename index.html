About & Research Focus

Short bio

I am a biologist with a strong interest in structural glycobiology and molecular recognition. During my PhD at the University of Naples Federico II, I have been working at the interface of wet-lab biophysics and computational modelling, focusing on Siglec-7 and its ligands.

Current research

My work integrates ITC, native mass spectrometry, NMR and molecular dynamics simulations to dissect the structural and thermodynamic basis of protein–glycan and protein–lipid interactions, with implications for immune regulation and host–pathogen interactions.

Selected Projects

Siglec-7 recognition of gangliosides

Structural and thermodynamic characterization of Siglec-7 binding to gangliosides, combining NMR protein assignment, ITC, fluorescence, MD simulations and native MS to understand how ligand flexibility shapes recognition.

Siglec-7 and microbial glycoconjugates

Investigation of Siglec-7 interaction with Neisseria meningitidis CPS and Fusobacterium nucleatum LPS O-antigens, linking molecular recognition to potential immune evasion mechanisms.
